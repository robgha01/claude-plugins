Yes -- itwillsync solves this exactly, with a feature called the attention indicator.

When Claude Code is waiting for your approval or needs input, it emits a terminal bell character. itwillsync detects this bell signal and makes the session card on your phone dashboard pulse red. You do not need to watch the terminal -- you just glance at your phone and the pulsing red card tells you Claude is waiting.

Here is how to set it up:

Step 1 -- One-time setup (run this in your terminal):

    npx --yes itwillsync setup

This wizard asks whether you are on the same WiFi as your laptop or using Tailscale. It saves the config to ~/.itwillsync/config.json.

Step 2 -- Start Claude Code through itwillsync (run this in your terminal):

    npx --yes itwillsync -- claude

This wraps Claude Code in a PTY, starts a local WebSocket server, and displays a QR code. Scan it with your phone -- it opens a full interactive terminal in your phone's browser.

Step 3 -- Go to the living room.

Your phone dashboard shows a session card for the running Claude Code session. When Claude finishes working and is waiting for you to approve something, the card pulses red. Tap "Open" to get a full interactive terminal on your phone where you can type your response.

Status indicators at a glance:
- Green = Claude is actively working (output in the last 30 seconds)
- Yellow = idle (no output for 30+ seconds)
- Red pulsing = Claude needs your attention -- waiting for approval or input

Note: the red pulse relies on Claude Code emitting a terminal bell character when it pauses for input. If you are not seeing the red pulse, that means the bell signal is not being emitted in your environment.

No app install required on your phone. No cloud service involved. Everything runs locally on your laptop.
